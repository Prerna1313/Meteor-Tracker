Deflection Systems Details

Gravity Tractor Method

A spacecraft positioned near an asteroid uses mutual gravitational attraction to slowly alter its trajectory over time.

Ion Beam Deflection

Directed ion thrusters create a continuous push on the asteroid surface, gradually changing its path.

Solar Sail Technology

Large reflective sails harness solar radiation pressure to provide continuous deflection force.

Kinetic Impactor Details

NASA DART Mission Success

The Double Asteroid Redirection Test successfully demonstrated kinetic impact technology by altering the orbit of Dimorphos asteroid.

Impact Velocity

Typical impactors travel at 6-10 km/s, delivering sufficient momentum to change asteroid trajectories.

Multi-Impactor Strategy

Multiple smaller impacts can be more effective than a single large impact for certain asteroid types.

NEO Survey Details

Pan-STARRS Telescope

Surveying the entire night sky every few days to detect moving objects and potential threats.

Catalina Sky Survey

Discovering more comets and NEOs than any other survey program.

ATLAS Project

Providing one-day warning for 30-meter asteroids and three-week warning for 45-meter asteroids.

Space-Based Observation Details

NEOCam Infrared Telescope

Space-based infrared telescope capable of detecting dark asteroids that are difficult to see from Earth.

Lagrange Point Observatories

Positioned at stable points in space to provide continuous monitoring without Earth's interference.

CubeSat Constellation

Network of small satellites providing wide-angle coverage of near-Earth space.
